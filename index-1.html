Interests — Theology

Theology

My first and deepest pursuit is God. Not religion as performance, but the search for truth about the One who made us.

I think often about the nature of God, the mystery of creation, the dignity and tragedy of the human soul, and what it means to live rightly before the divine. Much of my imagination and moral framework has been shaped by voices like Augustine of Hippo, whose restless search for God feels deeply human to me, and C. S. Lewis, who showed me that faith can be both intellectually serious and imaginatively rich.

Theology, for me, is not abstract study; it is the ground from which everything else in my life grows.

Interests — Books

Books

Books are my companions in thought and imagination. Through them I encounter worlds, ideas, and minds far beyond my immediate life.

Some books challenge my thinking, some deepen my faith, and others expand my sense of storytelling and human complexity. One work that has fascinated me for years is A Song of Ice and Fire by George R. R. Martin — not just for the spectacle of its world, but for its realism about power, human nature, and moral ambiguity.

Alongside works like Confessions and Mere Christianity, books help me think more deeply about life, faith, politics, and the human condition.

Interests — Philosophy

Philosophy

Philosophy is where I wrestle with the deeper questions of existence. Why are we here? What is truth? Why do people act the way they do? What makes a society flourish or decay?

I often find myself observing people — their mannerisms, their choices, their contradictions — and trying to understand the deeper structures beneath them. Philosophy sharpens my mind and forces me to question assumptions, both my own and those of the world around me.

It is a discipline of thinking carefully about reality.

Interests — Writing

Writing

Writing is my way of bringing everything together. It is where theology, philosophy, imagination, and lived observation meet.

Through storytelling I try to explore the spiritual and moral struggles that define human life. I am currently building a long-form story world beginning with my novel The Rest of Us, where themes of divine inheritance, spiritual decay, and the tension between sacred creation and artificial power are central.

Writing allows me to explore truths that are sometimes difficult to express directly. It is both creative work and a form of inquiry.

Interests — Career

Career

My professional path is accounting. I study and train in the discipline at Covenant University and prepare for the professional standards required by the ICAN.

While my intellectual interests range widely, I see career as a place where discipline, responsibility, and integrity are tested in the real world. It is the practical structure that allows me to serve society, support my ambitions, and build a life that integrates thought, faith, and contribution.
